Frequently asked questions

Explore these common queries to understand how Scripnote could fit into your financial journey.

What is Scripnote exactly?

Scripnote is an AI-powered financial advisor app that helps individuals and businesses manage finances with tools for budgeting, expense tracking, debt monitoring, and smarter financial planning.

Who is Scripnote meant for?

Scripnote is built for anyone looking to take control of their finances, from individuals managing personal money to businesses overseeing budgets, investments, and long-term strategies.

How much does Scripnote cost?

Scripnote costs $9.90 per month for individuals and $49.90 per month for businesses, with both plans offering full access to all features.

Is there a free trial available?

Yes. We offer a 14-day free trial for both individual and business accounts. No credit card is required.

Why isn’t there a free plan?

Scripnote doesn’t offer a free plan due to the resources required to maintain a secure and high-performing platform. Unlike free tools that may rely on ads or sell your data, we prioritize transparency and privacy. Our users know exactly what they’re paying for, with no hidden costs and no compromises on data security.

Why does the company plan cost more?

The business plan supports larger-scale operations with higher transaction volumes and more advanced features. It also includes exclusive financial education and insights tailored to company needs.

Can I connect my bank to Scripnote?

Currently, we do not support direct bank connections. Due to the privacy challenges associated with AI integration and the complexity of creating a seamless synergy between manual entries and automated transaction pulling, we have chosen to prioritize security and user control. However, we’re actively working on a feasible solution that we plan to introduce in version II.

How does Scripnote support financial sustainability?

Scripnote supports financial sustainability with AI-driven insights tailored to your goals. It helps individuals and businesses manage savings, reduce debt, and build long-term financial habits.
